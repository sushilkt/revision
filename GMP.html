STRATEGY

Stategy: Introduction

Strategy in simple words is A general direction set for the company and its various components to achieve a desired state in the future.
Strategic thinking should have 3 major elements a) forward and long term thinking b)system thinking c)consistency and alignment .
There are two parts of strategy corporate and business.
  • corporate strategy focusses on industry attractiveness
  • business strategy focusses on competitive advantage

Strategy formulation : defining business

Defining business : organisation from time to time need to ask and re-evaluate the business they are in , this is very crucial : eg: railways vs transportation, camera vs photography , steel vs materials.

Strategy formulation: situation analysis

Analysing current condition and future prospects at 4 levels, relationship between the levels is ever evolving . This analysis also helps us to identify driving forces , treats and opportunities for business. Firm having good understating is better positioned to take first mover advantage

  1. enviroment : PESTEL (political/Economic/social/technology/enviromental/legal)
  2. industry :porter 5 forces (byer power/suplier power/treat of substiture/new entrant/industry rivalry)
    • Porter 5 forces:
    • structure: monopoly/duoploy
    • conduct
    • performance
  3. Competitor:
    • n-firm
    • herfindal
    • strategic group
    • rivalry
    • SWOT: inwards (strength/weakness) :outwards(opportunity/threat)
  4. self:
    • vision/mission/vale: The vision, mission, and values statements form the foundation for all activities in an organization. The vision statement describes what the organization will become in the future. It is a broad and inspirational statement intended to engender support from stakeholders. The mission statement defines how the organization differentiates itself from other organizations in its industry. It is more specific than the vision statement and is intended to show how stakeholders’ needs will be satisfied. The values statement defines how people in the organization should behave. It provides a guideline for decision making.
    • value chain
      • primary activity:inbound /operation/outbound/marketing and sales/service
      • support activity: firm infrastructure/HR/technology/procurement
    • risk analysis

Strategy Formulation : Customer Segmentation and target

  • normal segmentation 4p/6p (product/place/price/promotion...and people/process)
  • blue ocean
  • crossing the chasm

Strategy Formulation: Sustainable Competetive Advantage

Finding sustainable competitive advantage ,based on resources and generic strategy adopted . this is one of the most crucial steps as sustainable competitive advantage enables the firm to stay ahead of competition and earn extra normal profits . Blue ocean strategy is sometimes used to create and capture new market segment
  • Resource Based
  • Porter Generic Startegy
    • Focus
    • Cost Leadership
    • Differentiation
  • Aggregation theory
  • VRIO(valuable/rarity/imitability/organization)
  • Canvas business model
